Digital Twin Layer

Every plant gets
an identity.

The connective tissue beneath every agent. From the moment a batch is propagated, Liventory creates a digital twin — a persistent, living record that ties together location, weather exposure, aging status, carbon data, and eventual sale. Not a dashboard. A living system that knows where everything is and what it's worth.

01
Batch twin created at propagation
The moment a new batch is started, a digital record is born — species, quantity, propagation date, growing zone. One entry up front. Everything after that is automatic.
02
Splits to individual records in the field
As plants move from greenhouse to field rows, each gets its own identity. A QR code on the tag you're already printing is all it takes to update it.
03
Agents read and write to every twin
Weather data attaches to the plant's zone. Aging flags trigger automatically. Inventory counts populate in real time. Every agent is connected to the same source of truth.
04
Twin travels with the tree — through sale
Traceability, care history, carbon sequestration data — all attached. Opens the door to verified provenance for premium buyers, online sales, and direct-to-customer markets.
📱
QR-Powered Field Updates
Workers or customers scan the tag already on the tree. Location updates, care logs, and inventory counts happen instantly — no spreadsheet, no desk, no delay.

Carbon Intelligence

More trees.
Better planet.
Better business.

Every tree a nursery grows is carbon pulled from the atmosphere. Liventory is being built to quantify that — and eventually turn it into grants, credits, and competitive advantage.

⚡
Tracked from operations
The goal is for emissions data to flow from fuel runs, deliveries, irrigation, and plant inventory with minimal manual entry — building a carbon record as a byproduct of running the business.
💰
Grant eligibility surfaced
USDA EQIP, CSP, and RCPP programs require documented emissions data. Liventory is designed to generate this as a byproduct of normal operations — making $10K–$50K in annual grants potentially accessible to qualifying nurseries.
📈
A future revenue stream: carbon credits
Voluntary carbon markets pay for verified sequestration. The long-term vision is for Liventory's carbon ledger to be the foundation for tree growers to participate in those markets — a revenue stream the industry has never had access to.
